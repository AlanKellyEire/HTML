About

Murphys Rock GAA Club is a Cork based Gaelic Athletic Association club based in the Rocky woods areas of Cork city, Ireland. The club was founded in 1876 and is primarily concerned with the game of hurling.

Brief History

The club has a long tradition of winning inter county trophies in the game of hurling and Gaelic football. Some past player to win the all Ireland with the cork senior team have been Thomas "Tommy" Daly, Nigel "Nidge" Delaney both in 1892, John "The Boy" Power, Eric "Elmo" Creed both in 1903. It took some 70 years for a Murphy's Rock player to win another all Ireland with the likes of Francis "Fran" Cooney and Adrian "Ado" Kenny in 1975. In resent time you may be familiar with the names Darren Treacy, Stephen "Stumpy" Doyle and Steven "tumpy" Doyle who all won inter county honour with Murphy's rock and also went on to win the All Ireland with cork.
